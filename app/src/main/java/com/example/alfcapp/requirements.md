Building a church app that people actually use requires moving beyond a "digital brochure." In 2025, the best apps act as a mid-week lifeline, not just a Sunday tool.

To make your app stand out, you should focus on three tiers: the "Must-Haves," the "Engagement Drivers," and the "Innovation" features.

1. The Core "Must-Haves"
   These are the foundational features every church app needs to be functional.

Integrated Media Player: Don't just link to YouTube. Use an in-app player for live streams and sermon archives with background audio so people can listen while driving or exercising.

One-Touch Giving: Make tithing seamless. Integration with Apple Pay, Google Pay, and recurring gift options significantly increases consistency.

Digital Bulletin & Interactive Notes: Replace paper bulletins with a digital version. Include fill-in-the-blank sermon notes that users can email to themselves or save to the cloud.

Centralized Calendar: A synced calendar where users can RSVP for events and add them to their personal phone calendars with one tap.

2. Features that Drive Daily Engagement
   To keep the app from being deleted, give people a reason to open it on a Tuesday morning.

Prayer Walls: A community space where members can post requests. A "I Prayed for This" button allows users to notify the requester that someone is standing with them in real-time.
